About Me


I am currently the Chief Researcher (主任) of Robot Software in the Basic Research Laboratory at FANUC Corporation, leading the research development of the next generation robot software (including but not limited to planning, control, teaching, learning, etc.) and the frontier robotic research collaboration with university.

I was a Ph.D student and then a Postdoctoral Scholar in the Mechanical Systems Control Lab in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at UC Berkeley. I worked with Professor Masayoshi Tomizuka on the several research projects including "A Hybrid Control Systems Approach to Brain-Machine Interfaces for Exoskeleton Control" and "Intelligent Control of Robot Manipulators for Performance Enhancement".

I obtained my M.S. and Ph.D. degrees both in Mechanical Engineering from UC Berkeley in May 2009 and August 2012, respectively. Prior to coming to UC Berkeley, I was an undergraduate student in Zhejiang University majored in Mechatronic Engineering and also hornored in Advanced Class of Engineering Education (ACEE), where I got my Bachelor degree in June 2007.
